## What are Custom Domains?

When deploying your applications on CodeRocket, you have two options:

1. **CodeRocket Subdomain** (Free): Use a subdomain like `yourapp.coderocket.app` - perfect for prototypes and testing
2. **Custom Domain** (Premium): Use your own domain like `app.yourdomain.com` - ideal for production and professional branding

Both options include automatic HTTPS/SSL certificates for secure connections.

<Card title="Premium Feature" icon="zap" color="#9333ea">
  Custom domains are available exclusively for premium users with deployed applications. [Upgrade your account](https://www.coderocket.app/pricing) to access this feature.
</Card>

Before adding a custom domain, make sure you have:

<Check>A CodeRocket premium subscription</Check>

## Choosing Your Domain Type

### During Initial Deployment

When you first deploy your application, you'll be asked to choose between:

- **CodeRocket Subdomain**: Free, instant setup, no configuration needed
- **Custom Domain**: Your own branding, requires DNS configuration (can be added after deployment)

<Tip>
  We recommend starting with a CodeRocket subdomain for quick testing, then adding a custom domain later for production.
</Tip>

## How to Add a Custom Domain

### Step 1: Deploy with CodeRocket Subdomain

First, deploy your application using a CodeRocket subdomain:

1. Navigate to your project
2. Open the **Deployment** tab
3. Select **CodeRocket Subdomain** as your domain type
4. Enter your desired subdomain (e.g., `myapp`)
5. Click **Deploy Application**

<Info>
  You must deploy your application with a CodeRocket subdomain before adding a custom domain.
</Info>

### Step 2: Add Your Domain

Once your application is deployed:

1. Scroll down to the **Custom Domain** section
2. Enter your domain name (e.g., `app.example.com` or `example.com`)
3. Click **Add Custom Domain**

<Tip>
  You can use either a root domain (`example.com`) or a subdomain (`app.example.com`). Subdomains are recommended for better flexibility.
</Tip>

### Step 3: Configure DNS Records

After adding your domain, you'll need to configure two DNS records:

#### TXT Record (for verification)

Add this record to verify domain ownership:

```text
Type: TXT
Host: _coderocket-verify.yourdomain.com (or _coderocket-verify.app.yourdomain.com)
Value: coderocket-verify-[your-unique-token]
TTL: 3600
```

<Note>
  The TXT record uses a subdomain prefix `_coderocket-verify` to avoid conflicts with the CNAME record. This is required by DNS standards and prevents errors on providers like OVH.
</Note>

#### CNAME Record (for routing)

Add this record to route traffic to CodeRocket:

```text
Type: CNAME
Host: yourdomain.com (or app.yourdomain.com)
Value: coderocket.app
TTL: 3600
```

<Note>
  DNS changes can take up to 48 hours to propagate, but usually complete within a few minutes.
</Note>

### Step 4: Verify Your Domain

After configuring the DNS records:

1. Wait a few minutes for DNS propagation
2. Click **Verify Domain** in the Custom Domain section
3. CodeRocket will check your DNS records automatically

<Success>
  Once verified, your domain will be marked as **Verified** and SSL certificate provisioning will begin automatically.
</Success>

## SSL Certificates

### Automatic HTTPS via Vercel

All custom domains automatically receive free SSL/TLS certificates powered by Vercel:

- ✅ **Automatic issuance** after domain verification
- ✅ **Powered by Let's Encrypt** (via Vercel)
- ✅ **Auto-renewal** before expiration
- ✅ **No configuration needed** - completely automatic
- ✅ **90-day validity** with automatic renewal

### How It Works

1. You verify your domain ownership (DNS TXT record)
2. CodeRocket automatically adds your domain to Vercel
3. Vercel provisions an SSL certificate
4. Your domain is accessible via HTTPS within minutes
5. Vercel automatically renews the certificate every 60 days

**No action required from you or your users!** 🎉

### Certificate Status

You can monitor your SSL certificate status in the Custom Domain section:

- **Pending**: Certificate is being issued by Vercel (usually takes 2-5 minutes)
- **Active**: Certificate is active and your domain is secure
- **Failed**: Certificate issuance failed (check DNS configuration)

<Note>
  Vercel handles all certificate renewals automatically. You will never need to manually renew certificates.
</Note>

<Warning>
  If certificate issuance fails, verify that your CNAME record correctly points to `coderocket.app` and try again.
</Warning>

## DNS Configuration Examples

### For a Subdomain (Recommended)

If you're using a subdomain like `app.example.com`:

```text
TXT Record:
  Name: _coderocket-verify.app.example.com
  Value: coderocket-verify-abc123...

CNAME Record:
  Name: app.example.com
  Value: coderocket.app
```

### For a Root Domain

If you're using a root domain like `example.com`:

```text
TXT Record:
  Name: _coderocket-verify.example.com (or _coderocket-verify if your provider uses relative names)
  Value: coderocket-verify-abc123...

CNAME Record:
  Name: example.com (or @)
  Value: coderocket.app
```

<Warning>
  **Important**: The TXT record must use the `_coderocket-verify` prefix. This prevents DNS conflicts because you cannot have both a TXT record and a CNAME record on the same exact subdomain.
</Warning>

<Info>
  Some DNS providers use `@` to represent the root domain, while others use the full domain name.
</Info>

## Common DNS Providers

Here's how to add DNS records with popular providers:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Cloudflare">
    1. Log in to your Cloudflare dashboard
    2. Select your domain
    3. Go to **DNS** > **Records**
    4. Click **Add record**
    5. Add the TXT and CNAME records
    6. Make sure the proxy status (orange cloud) is disabled for the CNAME record
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Namecheap">
    1. Log in to your Namecheap account
    2. Go to **Domain List** and select your domain
    3. Click **Advanced DNS**
    4. Add the TXT and CNAME records under **Host Records**
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="GoDaddy">
    1. Log in to your GoDaddy account
    2. Go to **My Products** > **DNS**
    3. Click **Add** to add new records
    4. Add the TXT and CNAME records
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Google Domains">
    1. Log in to Google Domains
    2. Select your domain
    3. Go to **DNS**
    4. Scroll to **Custom resource records**
    5. Add the TXT and CNAME records
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Managing Your Custom Domain

### Viewing Domain Status

In the Deployment tab, the Custom Domain section shows:

- Domain name
- Verification status
- SSL certificate status
- Last update time

### Updating Your Domain

To change your custom domain:

1. Remove the existing custom domain
2. Add a new domain
3. Configure DNS records for the new domain
4. Verify the new domain

<Warning>
  Removing a custom domain will make your application inaccessible at that domain. The old subdomain (`yourapp.coderocket.app`) will continue to work.
</Warning>

### Removing a Custom Domain

To remove a custom domain:

1. Go to the Deployment tab
2. Scroll to the Custom Domain section
3. Click the trash icon
4. Confirm the removal

## Troubleshooting

### Domain Verification Failed

If domain verification fails:

1. **Check DNS records**: Verify that both TXT and CNAME records are correctly configured
2. **Wait for propagation**: DNS changes can take time. Wait 15-30 minutes and try again
3. **Check DNS with a tool**: Use [DNS Checker](https://dnschecker.org/) to verify your records globally
4. **Remove cache**: Clear your browser cache and DNS cache

### SSL Certificate Pending

If your SSL certificate stays in "Pending" status:

1. Verify your domain is correctly verified
2. Check that the CNAME record points to `coderocket.app`
3. Wait up to 15 minutes for certificate issuance
4. If it persists, remove and re-add the domain

### Application Not Loading

If your application doesn't load on your custom domain:

1. Check that your application is deployed
2. Verify DNS records are correct
3. Check SSL certificate status
4. Try accessing via the original subdomain to verify the deployment works

<Tip>
  You can always access your application using the original CodeRocket subdomain even if custom domain configuration is pending.
</Tip>

## Best Practices

### Use Subdomains

Use a subdomain (like `app.example.com`) rather than your root domain:

- Easier to manage
- Better for multiple applications
- More flexible DNS configuration
- Recommended by DNS standards

### Monitor Certificate Expiration

While certificates auto-renew, monitor your certificate status:

- Check the Custom Domain section regularly
- Set up monitoring for your domain
- Renew failed certificates promptly

### Keep DNS Records Updated

- Don't delete DNS records while using custom domains
- Keep TTL values reasonable (3600 seconds recommended)
- Document your DNS configuration

## Limitations

- **One domain per project**: Each project can have only one custom domain
- **Verification required**: Domains must be verified before use
- **Premium only**: Custom domains require an active premium subscription
- **No wildcard domains**: Wildcard domains (`*.example.com`) are not supported

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I use multiple custom domains?">
    No, each project supports one custom domain. However, you can use both your custom domain and the CodeRocket subdomain simultaneously.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if I cancel my subscription?">
    If you cancel your premium subscription, your custom domain will stop working, but your application will remain accessible via the CodeRocket subdomain.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I transfer a domain to another project?">
    Yes, remove the domain from one project and add it to another. You'll need to re-verify the domain.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need to renew SSL certificates?">
    No, SSL certificates are automatically renewed before expiration. You don't need to do anything.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use a domain from any registrar?">
    Yes, as long as you can configure TXT and CNAME records for the domain, you can use it with CodeRocket.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
